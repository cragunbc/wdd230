Where is Syracuse located?


Syracuse, Utah, is located in the northern part of the state, just west of the Great Salt Lake. It is part of Davis County and is situated between the cities of Clearfield and Layton.

Statistical Information:


Population: According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the estimated population of Syracuse, Utah, as of 2020 is around 31,000 people.


Area: Syracuse has a total area of approximately 10.4 square miles (27 square kilometers), according to the U.S. Census Bureau.


Median Household Income: The median household income in Syracuse is around $93,000, based on 2020 data from the U.S. Census Bureau.


Median Home Value: The median home value in Syracuse is approximately $400,000, based on 2020 data from the U.S. Census Bureau.

What attractions does Syracuse offer?


Antelope Island State Park: Located nearby, this park offers stunning views of the Great Salt Lake, as well as opportunities for hiking, biking, wildlife viewing, and swimming.


Syracuse City Arts Council: This organization hosts various cultural events, including art exhibitions, concerts, and theatrical performances.


Syracuse Museum and Cultural Center: This museum showcases the history and culture of Syracuse and the surrounding area, with exhibits on local history, art, and more.


Parks and Outdoor Recreation: Syracuse boasts several parks and recreational areas, offering opportunities for picnicking, sports, and outdoor activities.
