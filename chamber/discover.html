Where is Syracuse located?


Syracuse, Utah, is located in the northern part of the state, just west of the Great Salt Lake. It is part of Davis County and is situated between the cities of Clearfield and Layton.

Statistical Information:


Population: According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the estimated population of Syracuse, Utah, as of 2020 is around 31,000 people.


Area: Syracuse has a total area of approximately 10.4 square miles (27 square kilometers), according to the U.S. Census Bureau.


Median Household Income: The median household income in Syracuse is around $93,000, based on 2020 data from the U.S. Census Bureau.


Median Home Value: The median home value in Syracuse is approximately $400,000, based on 2020 data from the U.S. Census Bureau.

What attractions does Syracuse offer?


Antelope Island State Park: Located nearby, this park offers stunning views of the Great Salt Lake, as well as opportunities for hiking, biking, wildlife viewing, and swimming.


Syracuse City Arts Council: This organization hosts various cultural events, including art exhibitions, concerts, and theatrical performances.


Syracuse Museum and Cultural Center: This museum showcases the history and culture of Syracuse and the surrounding area, with exhibits on local history, art, and more.


Parks and Outdoor Recreation: Syracuse boasts several parks and recreational areas, offering opportunities for picnicking, sports, and outdoor activities.

Early History of Syracuse


Settlement and Naming: Syracuse was settled in 1878 by pioneers from the LDS Church. The settlement was originally known as "Peach Valley" due to the abundance of peach orchards in the area. In 1883, the name was changed to Syracuse, inspired by the city of Syracuse in New York.


Agriculture: Like many early Utah settlements, Syracuse was primarily an agricultural community. The fertile soil and access to water from the nearby Great Salt Lake made it ideal for farming. The early settlers grew a variety of crops, including grains, fruits, and vegetables.


Transportation: The arrival of the railroad in the late 19th century had a significant impact on Syracuse. The railroad provided a means of transportation for goods and people, linking the town to larger cities and markets.


Community Growth: Syracuse grew steadily over the years, with new settlers arriving and the community expanding. Schools, churches, and other community institutions were established to serve the growing population.


Modernization: In the 20th century, Syracuse underwent modernization and development. The town saw the construction of new homes, businesses, and infrastructure to meet the needs of a growing population.


Today, Syracuse is a thriving community with a mix of residential, commercial, and agricultural areas. It retains its small-town charm while also offering modern amenities and services to its residents.

What is Syracuse famous for?


Antelope Island State Park: Syracuse is located near Antelope Island State Park, known for its stunning natural beauty, wildlife, and outdoor recreational activities. The park is home to a large herd of bison, hiking and biking trails, and beautiful views of the Great Salt Lake.


Outdoor Recreation: The Syracuse area offers a range of outdoor recreational opportunities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and boating. The nearby Great Salt Lake provides opportunities for swimming, sailing, and other water activities.


Family-Friendly Community: Syracuse is known for being a family-friendly community with excellent schools, parks, and recreational facilities. The town hosts events and activities throughout the year that bring the community together.


Rural Charm: Despite its proximity to larger cities like Salt Lake City, Syracuse has retained its rural charm. The town is surrounded by farmland and open spaces, giving it a peaceful, small-town feel.


Historical Significance: Syracuse has a rich history dating back to the mid-19th century when it was settled by pioneers. The town's historical sites and landmarks offer a glimpse into its past and the history of the region


Overall, Syracuse is known for its natural beauty, outdoor recreational opportunities, family-friendly atmosphere, and historical significance.

Facts about Syracuse


Name Origin: Syracuse was named after Syracuse, New York, by early settlers who were impressed by the salt production in both areas.


Peach Orchards: Syracuse was originally known as "Peach City" due to its thriving peach orchards in the late 19th century.


Antelope Island Connection: Syracuse is located near Antelope Island, the largest island in the Great Salt Lake, known for its wildlife and natural beauty.


Bridger Bay Beach: Syracuse is home to Bridger Bay Beach, a popular spot on Antelope Island known for its sandy beaches and clear waters, offering a unique beach experience near Salt Lake City.


Rodeo Heritage: The city has a strong rodeo heritage, with the Syracuse Rodeo being a popular event in the area.
